üéØ Target Audience

Who is your target audience? Be specific.

Anybody who likes music from the past 90 years.

What feedback have you gotten from potential users?

From our team: many of these exercise platforms exists but are plagued with a lot of "fluff": extraneous tools and flows that are a lot more complicated than we want. Many of these existing applications are free but are predicated deeply with subscription-based pricing models.

Others we have spoken to are longing for a tool that is straight-forward and does not get in the way of an efficient way to track fitness goals, with the potential for revenue-generating features beyond the initial MVP. These features should not get in the way of a user being able to quickly open the application and get started with their workout- whether performing or planning.

These applications are primarily mobile-based, whether as mobile versions of the web app or native. The former is our primary focus: we wish to develop a desktop and mobile version of the same application that can be seamlessly interacted with via the mobile version in the browser. Later down the line of further versions we can explore the potential of a native application for either Android or iOS but for right now we are only focused on the MVP functionality.

Have you validated this problem and your solution with a target audience? Describe how.

Somewhat: our group has asked friends and family about their experiences with these types of apps and the feedback is mixed. Some swear by their preferred product and others wish something simpler existed. We hope that we can solve the latter and develop a product that focuses on developing something that is capable to keep track and store exercise routines and details but is simple enough to be functional with a few adjustments. The less, the better, though this does not imply that this product is any less capable. The details that lay within a design that is smooth and effective is what we are most interested in.
